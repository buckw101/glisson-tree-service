Why It Matters

5 Reasons to Grind That Stump Now

🐛

Attracts Damaging Pests

Decaying stumps are prime habitat for termites, carpenter ants, and beetles. These pests can spread from the stump into your home's foundation and structure.

⚠️

Tripping & Injury Hazard

Stumps are invisible in tall grass and a serious injury risk, especially for children playing in the yard.

🌿

New Sprout Growth

Many stumps will continuously sprout new growth, creating an ongoing maintenance headache until the stump is completely removed.

🏡

Hurts Property Value & Curb Appeal

Stumps make your yard look neglected. Removing them instantly improves the appearance and value of your property.

🚜

Damages Lawn Equipment

Mowing too close to a stump can damage your mower's blades. Grinding it eliminates the problem completely.

FAQ

Stump Grinding Questions

Stump grinding pricing is primarily based on the stump diameter. We always provide a free estimate — call (478) 258-8169 or fill out the form below. Multiple stumps and bundles with tree removal qualify for discounts.
We typically grind 8–12 inches below the surface. This is deep enough to plant grass, lay sod, or install landscaping over. If you're planning to pour concrete or install a structure, let us know and we can grind deeper.
Grinding removes the stump and the root system at the base. While some lateral roots will remain underground, they'll decay naturally over time. Sprout regrowth from a properly ground stump is extremely rare.
You can choose to keep the grindings — they make excellent mulch for garden beds. Or we can haul them away as part of the cleanup. Just let us know your preference when we start the job.
