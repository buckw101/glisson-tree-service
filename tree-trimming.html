FAQ

Tree Trimming Questions Answered

Most established trees benefit from trimming every 2–4 years. Young trees may need more frequent attention to establish healthy structure. Trees near your home, power lines, or gutters may need annual clearance work. We'll assess your specific trees and give you a recommendation.
Trimming typically refers to shaping the outer canopy for aesthetics and clearance. Pruning is more targeted — selectively removing specific branches for health reasons (dead wood, disease, crossing branches). We combine both in most jobs to maximize both appearance and health.
Late fall through early spring (dormant season) is generally ideal for most species — trees are less stressed and wounds close faster. However, dead wood, hazardous branches, and storm damage should be removed immediately regardless of season. We work year-round in Macon.
Improper trimming — like topping or making flush cuts — can absolutely damage trees. Our crew follows ANSI A300 pruning standards, making proper cuts at branch collars that allow the tree to seal over wounds naturally and resist disease.
